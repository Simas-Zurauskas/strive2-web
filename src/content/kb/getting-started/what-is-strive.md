You arrive at Strive with a goal — *"I want to understand transformer neural networks"*, *"I'm prepping for the AWS Solutions Architect exam"*, *"I want to be conversational in Mandarin in three months"* — and a few honest sentences about what you already know. Strive's AI takes those answers and builds you a course shaped to your starting point and your destination. Not a generic syllabus. Yours.

## The core loop

Strive is built around a tight, repeatable cycle:

1. **Tell us what you want to learn.** A four-step wizard asks for your goal, your prior knowledge, the depth you want, and any specific outcomes you care about. A live AI agent helps you refine the structure before you commit.
2. **Generate the course.** Strive plans modules and lessons end-to-end. You see the structure first, approve it, then start studying.
3. **Study lessons on demand.** Each lesson is generated the moment you click into it — typed blocks of explanation, code, diagrams, math, and small inline checks stream onto the page block by block.
4. **Confirm understanding.** Two surfaces measure whether ideas are sticking: a *module quiz* at the end of each module that tests synthesis across lessons, and a daily *spaced-review queue* of small retrieval-practice cards extracted from your lessons.
5. **Repeat.** Add new courses, return to old ones, watch your library grow.

## What makes it different

A few things set Strive apart from other AI study tools:

- **Generation is on-demand, not pre-baked.** Lessons are written when you reach them, so the curriculum responds to where you are in the course rather than being one fixed PDF.
- **Two-layered assessment.** Module quizzes test cross-lesson understanding. The spaced-review queue keeps the atomic ideas from each lesson warm long after you've closed the page. Together, they apply two well-studied techniques — synthesis and retrieval practice — to actually move material into long-term memory.
- **Your answers shape the course, not a template.** The number of modules, the depth tier, and the specific lessons inside each module are derived from what you said in the wizard. A beginner wanting "an intro to React" gets a different course than a senior backend engineer wanting "React for someone who already knows the DOM cold".
- **Privacy is real.** Notes, bookmarks, and quiz attempts never feed back into generation, recommendations, or another learner's view.

## What Strive is not

Strive is not a tutor in your pocket that grades essays. It is not a flashcard app. It is not a course catalogue you browse. It is a personalized course generator — every course you study was built for *you*, in response to your specific goal.

If you have a topic you want to actually learn (not just sample), Strive is built for that.
